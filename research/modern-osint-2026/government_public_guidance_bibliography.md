## Key synthesis

- The strongest public methodology source is the Berkeley Protocol: planning, preservation, verification, legal/ethical considerations, and presentation ([GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations)).
- The strongest public US strategic source is the IC OSINT Strategy 2024–2026: OSINT as a discipline requiring governance, discovery, tradecraft, and data/AI modernization ([GOV-01: Intelligence Community OSINT Strategy 2024–2026](https://www.dni.gov/index.php/newsroom/reports-publications/reports-publications-2024/3897-the-ic-osint-strategy-2024-2026)).
- UK College of Policing material is valuable because it operationalizes proportionality, authorization, online research risk, and recordkeeping in a public law-enforcement context ([GOV-05: Open-source internet research](https://www.college.police.uk/app/investigation/investigative-strategies/internet-intelligence-and-investigations/open-source-internet-research), [GOV-06: Internet intelligence and investigations](https://www.college.police.uk/app/investigation/investigative-strategies/internet-intelligence-and-investigations)).
- EU/UK data-protection sources should inform any LLM skill that handles personal data, even when the immediate task is not in the EU/UK ([GOV-09: Guidelines 8/2020 on the targeting of social media users](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/guidelines/guidelines-82020-targeting-social-media-users_en), [GOV-10: Guide to the UK GDPR](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/)).
- Official registries are not just “sources”; they are part of the source hierarchy and confidence model. Use them before aggregators for corporate, sanctions, and regulatory records.
