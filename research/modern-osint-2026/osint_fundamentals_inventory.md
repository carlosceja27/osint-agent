# OSINT Fundamentals Inventory

Date: 2026-06-08

This artifact is designed for LLM ingestion. Each item states a principle, definition, operational indicators, failure modes, supporting sources, and implications for updating the existing OSINT skill.

| # | Fundamental | Definition | Operational indicators | Failure modes | Source support | Skill-update implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Requirements definition | Convert the user’s goal into a bounded intelligence question with mode, purpose, jurisdiction, subject, exclusions, and stopping conditions. | Written scope, source categories, geography, time period, must-not-collect list. | Fishing expeditions, overcollection, irrelevant sensitive data. | [GOV-01: Intelligence Community OSINT Strategy 2024–2026](https://www.dni.gov/index.php/newsroom/reports-publications/reports-publications-2024/3897-the-ic-osint-strategy-2024-2026); [GOV-05: Open-source internet research](https://www.college.police.uk/app/investigation/investigative-strategies/internet-intelligence-and-investigations/open-source-internet-research); [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations) | Add mandatory “collection requirements” before module selection. |
| 2 | Lawful open-source boundary | Use only publicly available or lawfully accessible sources; do not impersonate, deceive, bypass access controls, exploit systems, or access raw stolen data. | Public URL/registry/API; no login bypass; no pretexting; no exploit steps. | HUMINT/social engineering drift; CFAA/computer-misuse risk; breach-dump handling. | [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations); [GOV-05: Open-source internet research](https://www.college.police.uk/app/investigation/investigative-strategies/internet-intelligence-and-investigations/open-source-internet-research); [PRO-16: Have I Been Pwned](https://haveibeenpwned.com/) | Strengthen prohibited-actions list at top of skill. |
| 3 | Proportionality and minimization | Collect only what is necessary for the stated purpose, especially when personal data or private individuals are involved. | Relevance field for every finding; redaction of unnecessary home/contact/family details. | Doxxing risk, privacy harm, publication of unnecessary PII. | [GOV-09: Guidelines 8/2020 on the targeting of social media users](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/guidelines/guidelines-82020-targeting-social-media-users_en); [GOV-10: Guide to the UK GDPR](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/); [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations) | Add minimization test to every module and report section. |
| 4 | Source hierarchy | Prefer primary/official/original sources over aggregators, reposts, screenshots, or summaries. | Primary registry links; archive of original; aggregator used only for leads. | Publishing aggregator errors; circular citation; outdated profiles. | [GOV-11: Companies House Find and update company information](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/); [GOV-12: SEC EDGAR Full Text Search](https://www.sec.gov/edgar/search/); [PRO-06: OCCRP Aleph](https://aleph.occrp.org/); [PRO-07: ICIJ Offshore Leaks Database](https://offshoreleaks.icij.org/) | Label each source as primary/secondary/aggregator/tool-derived. |
| 5 | Identity resolution | Confirm that a record refers to the intended subject using multiple independent identifiers. | Exact name + location + date + affiliation + official ID; documented matching criteria. | Common-name collisions, sanctions/offshore false positives, wrong-person harm. | [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations); [PRO-07: ICIJ Offshore Leaks Database](https://offshoreleaks.icij.org/); [PRO-06: OCCRP Aleph](https://aleph.occrp.org/) | Add identity-resolution checklist and confidence labels. |
| 6 | Collection logging | Preserve how each finding was found, accessed, and interpreted. | Query, URL, access date, screenshot/archive, source tier, confidence, relevance. | Irreproducibility, broken links, unsupported claims. | [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations); [PRO-08: Hunchly: OSINT workflow and capture tool resources](https://hunch.ly/); [PRO-15: Internet Archive Wayback Machine](https://web.archive.org/) | Make evidence log mandatory; include query/navigation path. |
| 7 | Verification | Establish provenance, authenticity, date/time, location, and corroboration before stating conclusions. | Reverse search, archive checks, metadata, corroborating primary sources. | Treating viral content as true; manipulated images/video; quote misattribution. | [PRO-04: The Verification Handbook](https://verificationhandbook.com/); [PRO-05: First Draft Essential Guide to Verifying Online Information](https://firstdraftnews.org/long-form-article/essential-guide-to-verifying-online-information/); [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations) | Add verification checklist before final report. |
| 8 | Analytical uncertainty | Separate facts from inferences and assign confidence. | Confirmed/likely/possible/disputed/not found; alternative explanations. | Overclaiming, confirmation bias, false certainty. | [ACAD-06: Misinformation and Its Correction: Continued Influence and Successful Debiasing](https://doi.org/10.1177/1529100612451018); [ACAD-05: The spread of true and false news online](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aap9559); [ACAD-08: The science of fake news](https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aao2998) | Add uncertainty language templates and “what was not found.” |
| 9 | Archival preservation | Capture volatile web evidence where lawful and necessary. | Wayback/archive URL, screenshot hash where possible, snapshot date. | Link rot, changed pages, inability to reproduce. | [PRO-15: Internet Archive Wayback Machine](https://web.archive.org/); [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations) | Add archiving step for high-value findings. |
| 10 | Public-record literacy | Understand jurisdiction, record type, completeness, update cadence, and legal meaning. | Court/entity/filing metadata, jurisdiction notes, caveats. | Misreading filings, confusing charges with convictions, stale records. | [GOV-12: SEC EDGAR Full Text Search](https://www.sec.gov/edgar/search/); [GOV-11: Companies House Find and update company information](https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/); [GOV-16: Canada Business Registries search](https://beta.canadasbusinessregistries.ca/search); [GOV-17: Australian Business Register ABN Lookup](https://abr.business.gov.au/) | Add record-type caveats to public-record modules. |
| 11 | Platform/context literacy | Interpret social/platform data in context: impersonation, deletion, reposts, API limits, and algorithmic visibility. | Account history, handle changes, cross-platform continuity, original post link. | Mistaking reposts for origin; relying on follower counts. | [ACAD-03: Information Disorder: Toward an interdisciplinary framework for research and policymaking](https://rm.coe.int/information-disorder-report-november-2017/1680764666); [ACAD-04: Defining “Fake News”: A typology of scholarly definitions](https://doi.org/10.1080/21670811.2017.1360143); [PRO-04: The Verification Handbook](https://verificationhandbook.com/) | Add platform-context checks for social media module. |
| 12 | Automation control | Use tools for discovery and organization, not final truth. | Tool output logged; false positives reviewed; API/source limitations noted. | Tool bias, stale datasets, noisy entity graphs, overcollection. | [PRO-09: Maltego blog and resources](https://www.maltego.com/blog/); [PRO-11: SpiderFoot documentation](https://github.com/smicallef/spiderfoot); [ACAD-01: The Not Yet Exploited Goldmine of OSINT: Opportunities, Open Challenges and Future Trends](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9139412) | Add automation caveats and human validation gates. |
| 13 | Cyber OSINT safety | Limit technical OSINT to public exposure discovery and defensive analysis. | WHOIS/RDAP, DNS, CT logs, Shodan queries, advisories, threat reports. | Exploitation, credential abuse, scanning outside permission. | [PRO-10: Shodan Help Center](https://help.shodan.io/); [PRO-14: crt.sh Certificate Search](https://crt.sh/); [GOV-08: ENISA Threat Landscape 2024](https://www.enisa.europa.eu/publications/enisa-threat-landscape-2024) | Explicitly state “discovery only; no exploitation.” |
| 14 | AI-assisted analysis controls | Treat LLMs as assistants for planning, extraction, summarization, translation, and validation checklists, not as evidence. | Citations required; model outputs marked as synthesis; primary-source verification. | Hallucinated citations, fabricated facts, context leakage. | [GOV-01: Intelligence Community OSINT Strategy 2024–2026](https://www.dni.gov/index.php/newsroom/reports-publications/reports-publications-2024/3897-the-ic-osint-strategy-2024-2026); [ACAD-01: The Not Yet Exploited Goldmine of OSINT: Opportunities, Open Challenges and Future Trends](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9139412); [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations) | Add LLM-specific operating rules and acceptance checks. |
| 15 | Responsible reporting | Present findings, confidence, relevance, limitations, and next steps without enabling harm. | Executive summary, evidence table, redactions, unresolved questions, right-of-reply notes. | Doxxing, unsupported allegations, missing caveats. | [GOV-03: Berkeley Protocol on Digital Open Source Investigations](https://www.ohchr.org/en/publications/policy-and-methodological-publications/berkeley-protocol-digital-open-source-investigations); [PRO-04: The Verification Handbook](https://verificationhandbook.com/); [GOV-10: Guide to the UK GDPR](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/) | Strengthen final report templates. |
