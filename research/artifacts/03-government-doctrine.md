# Artifact 03 — Government Doctrine & Public Frameworks (US + NATO + Western-European NATO members)

> Purpose: inventory the **publicly available** government/intergovernmental doctrine on OSINT, so the skill can cite recognized frameworks. Geographic scope per request: **United States** and **NATO members in Western Europe (Türkiye excluded).** These are *what governments publish about how OSINT should be done* — useful as authority, terminology, and ethical/legal scaffolding.

---

## PART 1 — UNITED STATES

### 1.1 IC OSINT Strategy 2024–2026 (ODNI + CIA) — the flagship public doctrine
- Released **8 March 2024** by the Office of the Director of National Intelligence and the CIA. Subtitle/slogan: **"The INT of First Resort."**
- **Definition adopted:** OSINT = "intelligence derived **exclusively from publicly or commercially available information** that addresses specific intelligence priorities, requirements, or gaps" — and it "enables all other intelligence collection disciplines."
- **Strategic thrust / focus areas (public summary):**
  - **AI/ML & human-language technologies** are central; the IC "must expand and accelerate" their use "to sustain a competitive edge."
  - **Partner with industry and academia** to develop, test, and deploy OSINT tools and tradecraft.
  - **Generative-AI tradecraft** will "set the standards for the human–machine teaming that will be the foundation of OSINT in the future."
  - Emphasis on **data management, coordination across the IC, and tradecraft/workforce** development.
- **Governance:** the **CIA is the IC's OSINT Functional Manager** (see §1.3). Frames OSINT as foundational to *all* finished intelligence.

### 1.2 DIA OSINT Strategy 2024–2028 (Defense Intelligence Agency)
- The Defense Intelligence Agency's own public OSINT strategy, complementary to the IC strategy, covering defense/military OSINT.
- Themes (public reporting): OSINT as a **"foundational" element incorporated in nearly all finished intelligence products**; the explosion of digital/publicly available information as a "game-changing shift"; emphasis on **CAI, AI/ML, data, tradecraft/analytic standards, workforce, and integration with all-source intelligence.**
- *Naming note:* some 2025–26 references cite this under the renamed "Department of War"; cite the document as the **DIA OSINT Strategy 2024–2028** and verify the issuing-organization name against the current primary source.

### 1.3 Foundational IC policy lineage (terminology & governance)
- **Intelligence Community Directive (ICD) 301 — "National Open Source Enterprise"** (signed 2006 by DNI Negroponte): first established DNI policy for open-source activities, the Assistant Deputy DNI for Open Source, and the **DNI Open Source Center**. **Rescinded in 2012** — its functions absorbed under **ICD 113 (Functional Managers)**, which designates the **D/CIA as OSINT Functional Manager**. (Useful history; do not cite ICD 301 as current.)
- **Open Source Center (OSC) → Open Source Enterprise (OSE):** renamed **1 October 2015**; OSE is the IC's "center of excellence for open-source collection, analysis, and tradecraft." (OSC was the successor to the famous **FBIS — Foreign Broadcast Information Service**, the historical root of US OSINT.)
- **DoD Instruction 3115.12, "Open Source Intelligence (OSINT)":** the standing DoD policy issuance — defines OSINT, assigns roles/responsibilities, and ties OSINT to the all-source process and to **intelligence-oversight / privacy & civil-liberties** rules (US-person protections).
- **Analytic-standards backbone (applies to OSINT analysis):** **ICD 203 — Analytic Standards** (objectivity; independence of political consideration; timeliness; based on all available sources; and tradecraft standards incl. **proper sourcing**, expression of **uncertainty/confidence**, distinguishing assumptions from judgments, consistency, accuracy, logical argumentation) and **ICD 206 — Sourcing Requirements**. These are the closest the US has to a public "how to reason and source" standard and map cleanly onto OSINT analysis.

### 1.4 Commercially Available Information (CAI) — ODNI Senior Advisory Group report (June 2023)
- DNI Avril Haines commissioned, then **declassified (12 June 2023)**, a Senior Advisory Group report on the IC's purchase/use of **CAI**.
- **Findings:** the IC acquires a "significant amount" of CAI; CAI "can reveal sensitive and intimate information about individuals"; agencies often **did not know how much CAI they held, of what type, or how it was used**; raises both **counterintelligence** and **US-person privacy/civil-liberties** risks.
- **Recommendations:** catalog IC CAI use/acquisition; create standards & procedures; develop "more precise sensitivity- and privacy-protecting guidance."
- Why it's in scope: data-broker / people-search collection (the skill's Module F) **is** CAI. This report is the government's own statement that "buyable ≠ ethically free." (See Artifact 07.)

## PART 2 — NATO (intergovernmental)

### 2.1 NATO OSINT Handbook (2001) + companion volumes
- The **NATO Open Source Intelligence Handbook v1.2 (Jan 2002)**, published by Supreme Allied Command Atlantic (SACLANT), with the **NATO OSINT Reader** and **Intelligence Exploitation of the Internet** — the publicly available foundational OSINT doctrine set.
- Lasting contributions: the **OSD → OSIF → OSINT → Validated OSINT** taxonomy (Artifact 01 §2); framing OSINT's support to the **all-source process** and to unclassified needs of operators, logisticians, and civil/coalition partners; "legally and ethically obtained" emphasis. Dated on tools, durable on concepts.
- **NATO AJP‑2.1** (Allied Joint Doctrine for Intelligence Procedures) carries the **source-reliability (A–F) / information-credibility (1–6)** grading scales (Artifact 01 §5).

### 2.2 NATO's 2024 modernization push (the "cultural change")
- 2024 NATO messaging (Breaking Defense; Atlantic Council; RUSI Journal 2025): OSINT needs a **"cultural change"** — militaries historically dismissed it as untrustworthy/manipulable; leaders now argue **up to ~90% of what Western militaries seek "can be gleaned out of open source."**
- **Exercise Northern Raven (May 2024, Finland):** **11 NATO nations, ~30 analysts**, executing a collection plan drafted six months prior — described as the **largest OSINT collection operation in NATO history**. The **Army Europe Open Source Center (AEOSC, Wiesbaden)** coordinates collection requirements.
- **NATO School Oberammergau** runs a formal **NATO OSINT course (N2‑04)** — evidence of institutionalized OSINT training.

## PART 3 — WESTERN-EUROPEAN NATO MEMBERS (Türkiye excluded)

### 3.1 United Kingdom — the most developed public law-enforcement OSINT doctrine
- **College of Policing / NPCC "Internet Intelligence and Investigations" framework.** Splits online activity into **Overt** vs **Covert**, and overt into **"Core Internet Use"** (ad-hoc) vs **"Internet Investigations"** (structured, by trained professionals). Trained investigators must be able to **evidentially capture and store** material and submit products into force intelligence systems.
- **"JAPAN" principles** — every online research action should be **Justified, Authorised, Proportionate, Auditable, Necessary** — operationalizing **Human Rights Act / ECHR Article 8** (privacy).
- **NPCC Guidance on Open Source Investigation/Research (2023)** — national police guidance.
- **Legal thresholds (critical, and transferable):** under **RIPA 2000** and the **Investigatory Powers Act 2016**, online research crosses into regulated **directed surveillance** or a **Covert Human Intelligence Source (CHIS, RIPA s.29)** when the investigator **builds a "pattern of life," monitors an individual over time, or systematically extracts and retains a record** — or when they **interact** under a false persona to cultivate a relationship. **The threshold is low.** Authorizations require **necessity and proportionality.** → A clean public articulation of *when "just looking" becomes surveillance.*
- Academic/practitioner commentary: Sheffield Hallam "OSINT from a UK perspective"; osint.uk "Laws and Ethics for Government OSINT"; Proelium Law (GDPR + OSINT).

### 3.2 European Union (Union-level bodies; most members are NATO)
- **Europol:** stood up a dedicated **OSINT taskforce (2023)** to support **Ukraine war-crimes** investigations via collection/analysis of OSINT; uses OSINT + **SOCMINT** operationally (e.g., fugitive tracing via ENFAST). Concept paper **"Policing in an Online World."**
- **CEPOL** (EU law-enforcement training agency): recurring **"OSINT and IT Solutions"** courses and **train-the-trainer** programs — the EU's standardized LE OSINT curriculum.
- **EU Intelligence and Situation Centre (EU INTCEN):** the EU's civilian intelligence body; historically grew from a Joint Situation Centre that fused **open-source material**, member-state intelligence, and diplomatic reporting (France, Germany, Italy, Netherlands, Spain, Sweden, UK among contributors).
- **Legal frame:** **GDPR** governs processing of EU residents' personal data **regardless of investigator location**; the **EU AI Act** adds transparency/risk obligations for AI systems and reaffirms data-protection rights; **EU Charter Article 7** (privacy) ≈ **ECHR Article 8**. (See Artifact 07.)

### 3.3 France, Germany, Netherlands, Nordics (selected national signals)
- Public, document-level national OSINT doctrine is **less openly published** here than in the US/UK, but adoption is well-attested:
  - **France:** "renseignement d'origine sources ouvertes (ROSO)"; growing military/defense OSINT integration; commercial OSINT among the largest European markets.
  - **Germany:** significant defense/security OSINT adoption; among Europe's largest OSINT markets; active in NATO/EU OSINT coordination.
  - **Netherlands, Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Estonia, Poland, Finland:** repeatedly cited for **advanced technical/cyber and OSINT capabilities** and for piloting **online-crime OSINT** programs; Finland hosted **Northern Raven 2024**; Bellingcat is **Netherlands-based**.
- For named **France/Germany** national doctrine, treat the above as *context*; the publicly citable, document-grade European frameworks are **UK (College of Policing/NPCC)** and **EU (Europol/CEPOL)**.

## What the doctrine *agrees* on (the citable consensus)

1. OSINT is **foundational**, not supplementary — "first resort," enabling all other disciplines.
2. **AI/ML + human–machine teaming** is the strategic future (US IC, DIA, NATO).
3. OSINT must be **lawful, ethical, proportional, and auditable**, with explicit **privacy / civil-liberties** safeguards (DoDI 3115.12; UK JAPAN/RIPA; GDPR/AI Act).
4. **CAI/data-broker data is a distinct, heightened-risk category** (ODNI 2023).
5. **Analytic standards apply:** objectivity, proper sourcing, expressed uncertainty/confidence, source reliability & information credibility grading (ICD 203/206; NATO AJP-2.1).
6. **Validation/corroboration** separates OSINT from open-source *information* (NATO taxonomy).

## Implications for the `osint_audit` skill

- The skill can **cite recognized authority** for principles it already follows: ICD 203 analytic standards (sourcing, confidence), NATO grading, UK JAPAN/proportionality, GDPR.
- Its **legal section is US-only**; it should add **UK (JAPAN, RIPA/IPA thresholds), EU (GDPR extraterritorial reach, EU AI Act)** given the requested European scope.
- The **UK "when looking becomes surveillance" threshold** is a concrete, defensible rule the skill currently lacks — valuable for both journalism and self-audit modes (e.g., "monitoring over time / building a pattern of life raises the bar").
- The skill's data-broker module should reference the **ODNI CAI findings** as the ethical rationale for caution/minimization, not just opt-out logistics.
